### Page 9

*   **Content Description:** This page provides the formulas used to calculate the generation time of a bacterial culture.
*   **Visible Text:**
    *   **Header:** "Calculation of generation time" (in bold red font).
    *   **Definitions of variables (in red font):**
        *   "t₀: time at which the number of cells is N₀"
        *   "t: time at which the number of cells is Nₜ"
        *   "n: number of cell generations in the (t-t₀) time interval"
    *   **Formulas (centered, in bold red font):**
        *   "Nₜ = N₀. 2ⁿ" (Represents exponential growth, where the final cell number equals the initial cell number multiplied by 2 raised to the power of the number of generations.)
        *   "logNₜ = logN₀ + n.log2" (The logarithmic form of the growth equation, useful for linearization.)
        *   "n = (logNₜ – logN₀)/log2" (Rearranged to calculate the number of generations 'n' from the initial and final cell numbers.)
        *   "g = (t-t₀)/n" (Defines generation time 'g' as the total time elapsed divided by the number of generations.)
        *   "g = [log 2 * (t – t₀)]/(logNₜ – logN₀)" (A combined formula to directly calculate generation time 'g' using time points and cell numbers, without explicitly calculating 'n' first. Note: log here implies log₁₀, and log 2 ≈ 0.301).
*   **Images/Diagrams:** There are no images or diagrams on this page.

### Page 10

*   **Content Description:** This page presents a practical example demonstrating the calculation of bacterial generation time using the formulas provided on the previous page.
*   **Visible Text:**
    *   **Header:** "Calculation of generation time" (in bold red font).
    *   **Example Problem (in black font):** "Example: A fermenter with bacteria in the growth phase contains 10⁴ cells/ml at 6h and 10⁸ cells/ml at 10h. Calculate the generation time."
    *   **Solution Steps (centered, in bold red font):**
        *   "g = [log 2 * (t – t₀)]/(logNₜ – logN₀)" (The relevant formula is reiterated.)

        *   "g = 0,301 (h)" (The final result of the calculation: 0.301 * 4 / 4 = 0.301 hours.)
