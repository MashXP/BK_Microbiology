## ðŸ§  Core Concepts (The Deep Dive)

### 1. Feedstock: Why Azolla?
*   **Rapid Growth:** *Azolla* is one of the fastest-growing plants on Earth.
*   **Symbiosis:** It fixes its own nitrogen through a relationship with the cyanobacterium *Anabaena azollae*.
*   **Sustainable:** Can be grown in wastewater, avoiding competition with food crops and reducing nutrient pollution.

### 3. Yeast Comparison & Fermentation
Not all yeasts are equal in their ability to make fuel.

| Yeast Species | Fermentation Traits | Performance on *Azolla* |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Kluyveromyces marxianus** | Thermotolerant; consumes **both** Glucose and Xylose. | **Best:** Highest ethanol yield (YEtOH = 0.43). |
| **Candida lusitaniae** | Good at fermenting xylose. | Second best performer. |
| **Pichia stipitis** | Naturally ferments xylose, but inhibited by high glucose. | Moderate performance. |
| **Saccharomyces cerevisiae** | Classic brewer's yeast; **cannot** ferment xylose naturally. | **Lowest:** Only used the glucose, leaving xylose wasted. |

> **The Xylose Rule:**
> For lignocellulosic biomass (which is high in xylose), a yeast like **K. marxianus** is superior because it prevents sugar waste, leading to a much higher overall ethanol yield.

## âš ï¸ Common Pitfalls
*   **Sugar Degradation:** If acid hydrolysis is too long (>60 min), sugars can degrade into inhibitors like HMF or furfural, which kill the yeast.
*   **Glucose Inhibition:** Many yeasts won't touch xylose until all the glucose is gone (Diauxic growth).
*   **Viscosity:** Slurry concentrations above 14% become too thick to mix effectively, reducing efficiency.
