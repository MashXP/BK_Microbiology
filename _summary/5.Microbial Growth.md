## üìà The Requirements for Growth

### Physical Requirements

#### 1. Temperature
-   **Psychrophiles**: Cold-loving microbes.
-   **Mesophiles**: Moderate-temperature-loving microbes.
-   **Thermophiles**: Heat-loving microbes.
-   **Hyperthermophiles**: Extreme heat-loving microbes.

#### 2. pH
-   Most bacteria grow well at a pH between 6.5 and 7.5.
-   Very few bacteria can grow at a pH below 4.
-   Most yeasts and molds grow well at a pH between 5 and 6.
-   Culture media are often supplemented with chemical buffers like peptone, amino acids, or phosphate salts to maintain a stable pH.

#### 3. Osmotic Pressure
-   Bacteria require water for growth.
-   High osmotic pressure (a hypertonic environment) removes water from the cell, causing the cytoplasm to shrink (plasmolysis) and inhibiting growth.
-   **Extreme halophiles** require high salt concentrations for growth.
-   **Facultative halophiles** can tolerate high salt concentrations (up to 2% or even 15%) but do not require them.

### Chemical Requirements

#### 1. Carbon
-   Carbon is the structural backbone of all organic compounds in a cell.
-   Half the dry weight of a typical bacterial cell is carbon.
-   **Chemoheterotrophs** get carbon from organic sources like proteins, carbohydrates, and lipids.
-   **Chemoautotrophs** and **photoautotrophs** get carbon from carbon dioxide (CO‚ÇÇ).

-   Essential organic compounds that an organism cannot synthesize on its own.
-   Examples include vitamins (which often act as coenzymes), amino acids, purines, and pyrimidines.

#### 5. Oxygen
-   **Obligate Aerobes**: Require oxygen for life.
-   **Facultative Anaerobes**: Can use oxygen but can also grow without it using fermentation or anaerobic respiration.
-   **Obligate Anaerobes**: Are unable to use oxygen and are harmed by it.
-   **Aerotolerant Anaerobes**: Cannot use oxygen for growth but tolerate it. They have enzymes like superoxide dismutase (SOD) to neutralize toxic oxygen forms.

## üß™ Culture Media

-   A **culture medium** is a nutrient material prepared for the growth of microorganisms.
-   An **inoculum** is the microbes introduced into a medium to initiate growth.
-   A **culture** is the microbes that grow and multiply in or on the medium.

### Criteria for a Culture Medium:
-   Must contain the right nutrients.
-   Must have sufficient moisture, a proper pH, and a suitable oxygen level.
-   Must be initially sterile.
-   Must be incubated at the proper temperature.

### Types of Media

-   **Agar**: A complex polysaccharide from seaweed used as a solidifying agent. It melts at ~100¬∞C and solidifies at < 40¬∞C.
    -   **Deep agar**: Solid agar in a test tube.
    -   **Slant agar**: Solid agar in a test tube that has been allowed to solidify at an angle.
    -   **Agar plate**: Solid agar in a petri dish.
-   **Chemically Defined Media**: The exact chemical composition is known.
-   **Complex Media**: Made of nutrients from extracts of yeasts, meat, or plants. The exact chemical composition varies.
-   **Reducing Media**: Contain ingredients (like sodium thioglycolate) that chemically combine with and deplete dissolved oxygen. Used for growing anaerobic bacteria.
-   **Selective Media**: Suppress the growth of unwanted bacteria and encourage the growth of desired microbes.
-   **Differential Media**: Make it easier to distinguish colonies of a desired organism from others on the same plate.
-   **Enrichment Media**: A liquid medium that provides nutrients and conditions to favor the growth of a particular microbe that may be present in small numbers.

## üî¨ Obtaining Pure Cultures

-   A **colony** is a visible mass of microbial cells that theoretically arises from a single spore or vegetative cell.
-   A **pure culture** contains only one species or strain.
-   The **streak plate method** is the most common method used to isolate pure cultures. It involves spreading a small amount of inoculum over the surface of an agar plate in a way that individual cells are separated from each other.
