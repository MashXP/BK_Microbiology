### Page 44

*   **Main Header:** Bacterial cell preservation
*   **Section (indicated with a small circle): Long-term:**
    *   **Point 1 (Filled dot): Deep freezing method:** Uses a bacterial suspension solution (glycerol 10-30%) followed by quick freezing at -70 °C to -95 °C. This allows for subculturing after several years.
    *   **Point 2 (Filled dot): Freeze-drying method (Lyophilization):** A multi-step process described with arrows: Bacteria-containing medium (emulsion, serum, or sodium glutamate) → placed in a capsule → rapid cooling (-54 °C to -72 °C) → water separation by vacuum machine → sealed → stored in a refrigerator. This method allows for preservation for 10 years or longer.
